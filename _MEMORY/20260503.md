## Performance Optimization

**3-layer optimization for `tv.check()`:**

| Layer | Mechanism | Effect |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| 1. 30s guard | Skip if `now - _last_checked_now < 30s` | **0.4µs** skip |
| 2. Dedup `_last_ts` | Skip indicator compute if 5m bar unchanged | **save 642ms** per skip |
| 3. Incremental fetch | Cache df, only query new rows since `_cached_end` | **save 365ms** per skip |

**Benchmark (24h live, check every 1 min):**

| Path | Before | After | Speedup |
|------|--------|-------|---------|
| Cold (first call) | ~1000ms | **379ms** | 2.7× |
| Skip (no new 5m) | ~365ms | **23ms** | 16× |
| Hit (new 5m bar) | ~1000ms | **286ms** | 3.5× |

**24h live overhead:** 101s/day = 0.12% CPU (was ~24 min/day).
**Walkforward batch:** 2.98s for 2 days (was ~60s via replay engine).

**Known bottleneck remaining:** `_save_signals()` called per signal writes growing JSON to disk. Only impacts first cold call (290 historical signals). Not a live issue (1 signal/min max).

## What NOT to do

- Do not confuse `walkforward_telegram.py --batch` (direct loop, ~3s) with `--incremental` (tv.check(), ~60s+)
- Do not rename `telegram.env` without restoring — SignalBus silently fails
- Do not use `<` vs `<=` incorrectly in `feed_bulk()` — overlap causes UNIQUE constraint
- Do not forget `label="right"` in resample — timestamps shift 5 min
- Do not modify `_store_signal()` signature without updating `bus_payload` structure
